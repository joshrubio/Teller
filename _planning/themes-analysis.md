## 1. Looking has a cost

The spine of the serial. Oliver's Registro is a cumulative cost the text documents with precision.

| Cap | Event | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| 13 | Involuntary copy of Asha | Cracked panel, loss of control |
| 17 | First overflow of Riku's field | Radius exceeded, object bent in corridor |
| 24 | Passive processing threshold without return | Active signatures without Oliver initiating |
| 28 | Involuntary copy of Strength/Inertia | Kinetic saturation, first real physical cost |
| 31 | 14 minutes of continuous Registro | Complete collapse |
| 32 | Kaspar describes the cost from inside | 40 years of accumulation as mirror |

---

## 2. The institution vs. the speed of people

No villain. AMAT is not malicious; it is slow. Voss, Cap 37: *"No fue una falla de seguridad. Fue una falla de velocidad."*

Evidence: 9 months without approved methodology (Cap 35). Tomás sent before he could choose (Cap 18). Sisi in containment 9 months without review threshold (Cap 22). Registro protocol took 40 years to improve.

---

## 3. Copying is not owning

Oliver has Sabine's Domain when Sabine leaves. Riku says "Sabine" in Cap 37. Dara's arc: if the Double dies in the original's place, who survived? Book 2: Oliver cannot copy Elise's trajectory.

## 5. Loyalty as subtext

Park nods in the north corridor (Cap 35) — that was enough. Oliver says "Vuelve" (Cap 36) — not "te quiero." "La ciudad ha seguido funcionando." (Cap 20) — the only acknowledgment at reunion. Riku: "¡Oliver!" — first time fear. Oliver did not look back.

Model: David and Jonathan (1 Samuel 18–20) for Oliver/Park.
