# Hartford Zone

## Classification

Principado imprint — active since 1952 withdrawal.

## Location

Hartford, Connecticut, USA. Four city blocks of historic district cordoned by AMAT-NA in 1952. Oldest active perimeter in AMAT-NA.

## Status

Active impregnated zone. AMAT-NA perimeter under environmental remediation cover (generic: ZONA DE RIESGO AMBIENTAL / ACCESO RESTRINGIDO). The Principado withdrew — destination unknown. Generates Nivel 3–4 Potestades periodically.

## Physical description (from Cap 11)

- Perimeter fencing is generic and regularly replaced; the *line* hasn't moved since 1952.
- Buildings inside still standing but unoccupied since 1952. Ground-floor businesses shuttered.
- Windows have a color glass shouldn't have (canonical detail — do not over-explain).
- Aerial photo from 1952: radial pattern on asphalt in the north sector. Not explosion damage — saturation. The ground absorbed something it didn't know how to return.

## AMAT-NA base

The operational base occupies the first two floors of a commercial brick building on the eastern edge of the cordoned perimeter — one of the original 1952 structures, claimed by AMAT-NA when the perimeter was established. Never returned. From outside it still looks like the import company whose name is faded on the facade.

- Technically inside the cordon (perimeter fence passes 3 meters from the north facade).
- Ground floor: monitoring stations, 8–9 technical staff, real-time sector readouts. Sector map always shows north in red, south orange, rest yellow — no green on any zone map.
- Second floor: coordination room, field team briefings. Capacity ~14–16.
- Staff who work here long-term adapt to the zone's ambient conditions. New arrivals typically notice a difference on entry without being able to name it precisely.

## AMAT protocol

Long-term perimeter monitoring. No containment attempt of the zone itself. Three-unit response per event: **Exploración** (locates entity, confirms influence radius, exits) → **Eliminación** (direct contact, neutralizes — Nivel 3 requires contact, no remote option) → **Disposición** (cleanup). 72-year unbroken record until Park's Cap 11 displacement.

**Alarm system:** internal base signal — not loud, doesn't need to be. Everyone knows the drill. Full mobilization within seconds. Canonical: activated end of BSA Cap 1, cause not confirmed by end of chapter.

**Briefing room:** second floor, confirmed ~16 people. Half veteran, half new rotation. Hendricks runs operational briefings using a zone-adapted localizador — variant of the standard AMAT dispositivo, live sensor feed instead of file access. Veterans bring their own dispositivos to briefings.

## Narrative function

- Park's M4 (Hartford, BSA Arc 1) takes place here — team operation under special zone classification. Credit shared; does not count as clean solo resolution.
- The zone itself is the threat, not a single entity — a permanent scar.
- Distorts Domain function for anyone operating inside.
- Park reads the 1952 Hartford file immediately after receiving the M4 assignment (~1–2 weeks post-Grayhaven, before Cap 11). Pages 74+ classified under EU-Ginebra — inaccessible at his clearance level.

## Connection to Kaspar

Hartford zone was active during Kaspar's AMAT custody period (~1982). The Principado's imprint and Kaspar's extended Domain use run parallel as themes of long-term accumulation.
