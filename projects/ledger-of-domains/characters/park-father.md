## Physical description

Tall — same height as Park Jun-ho or slightly taller. Dark hair with gray beginning at the temples, more pronounced in recent years. The face is difficult to read: not blank, but managed — the difference between a man with no expression and a man who has chosen one. Eyes that inventory a room when they enter it. When he looks at someone directly it is a decision, not a reflex.

Bearing: the same angle in the shoulders whether standing or seated. Economy in every movement — nothing wasted, nothing hurried. In the office: jacket off, sleeves rolled to the elbow. Same watch Park has seen since childhood.

**Reference:** Gendo Ikari (Neon Genesis Evangelion) — the silhouette, the stillness, the quality of attention that makes the room reorganize around where he stands.

## Personality / Dynamic with Park

Institutional first. Park is inventory before he is son. What matters about Park Jun-ho is what is in the record — credits, rank, resolution history — and what can be used. Not out of cruelty: out of a framework in which performance is the only language that survives intact.

The Gendo/Shinji dynamic: his care, if it exists, expresses itself through placement and opportunity, not through warmth. He arranges things. He doesn't explain why he arranges them. Park infers.

**What he doesn't do:** explain his mechanisms. He announces results and lets Park calculate the rest.

**What Park wants from him and doesn't get:** one response that isn't about the record. One second where the arm in cabestrillo prompts something that isn't operational. Park filters this want out before it reaches the surface; the reader sees what Park filtered.

## Relationship with Ji-ho

Ji-ho is his brother-in-law or brother (Park family — exact relation TBD). The father and Ji-ho are in coordination about Park specifically — Ji-ho followed Hartford from Seoul, and the father is aware of this. The exact nature of their alignment (collaborative, competitive, complementary) is TBD.

## Relationship with Cartwright

Friends since before Park Jun-ho was born. Called in a favor to arrange Park's transfer to Seoul (BSA Cap 6). Cartwright approved the transfer; the father designed the operational rationale.
