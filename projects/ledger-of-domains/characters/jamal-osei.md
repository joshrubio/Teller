## Domain — Contención

Osei creates planar Odem membranes that resist movement in one direction. The membrane is one-way: it allows passage from one side and blocks it from the other. Multiple membranes can be layered to create containment zones.

**Standard use:** seal dispersal routes, block entity retreat vectors, hold a perimeter once contact is established. The membrane does not need to be maintained actively — once placed, it holds until Osei collapses it or his Odem reserve depletes.

**Hartford-specific risk:** the membranes do not discriminate between entity Odem and ambient Odem. In a zone with active saturation, a broadly placed membrane interacts with the saturated field itself — with Saturación specifically, whose Odem IS the ambient field. A wide membrane in Hartford risks either failing (the ambient Odem cancels the membrane's directional property) or producing unpredictable interaction effects with Saturación's distributed presence.

This means Osei's membranes must be placed with surgical precision in Hartford — narrow, targeted, at verified entity-Odem concentrations rather than sector-wide deployment. Hendricks brought him for the containment capability and gave him a narrow radius of operation for this exact reason.

## Personality

Methodical. Understands his Domain's limitations in saturated environments better than most agents understand their own. Does not overextend — his calibration of what the field can support in Hartford is accurate.

## Narrative function in BSA

Arc 1 (Hartford). Containment layer once contact is established. His constraint in Hartford — the non-discrimination problem — creates tactical friction: he can seal routes but only after Reyes or Bauer confirms entity-specific Odem location, not sector saturation.
