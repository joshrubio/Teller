## Domain — Kinetic redistribution

On impact, Cho's Domain redistributes incoming kinetic force across his Odem field rather than allowing it to concentrate at the point of contact. A blow that would fracture a skull instead fractures three ribs. A blow that would fracture three ribs leaves bruising. The force is not absorbed or nullified — it is dispersed across a larger surface area via the Odem.

Secondary application: he can store incoming kinetic energy in his Odem field rather than immediately dispersing it, then release it as directed force output. This requires timing and concentration — absorb on impact, hold, release on strike. Used correctly, the harder he is hit, the harder he can hit back. Not automatic; deliberate.

**Ceiling:** there are thresholds of force the Domain cannot fully redistribute. What the Saturación entity did in BSA Cap 2 was near that ceiling — Cho survived, but barely. The gap between survived and didn't is not a comfortable margin.

**Relation to Park's Domain:** both operate in the force/inertia axis. Force-axis Domains are common in Korean and Korean-diaspora AMAT agents — AMAT-Korea's operational data shows a disproportionate frequency. Cho and Park's Domains share a mechanical family, different implementations.

## Personality

Processes damage as operational data, not alarm — consistent with his Domain's logic. In the field: describes injury the same way he describes weather. "Dos costillas, probablemente. Funciona."

Not talkative under fire. Communicates in confirmations.

## Narrative function in BSA

Arc 1 (Hartford). Park's field partner across Caps 1–2 at minimum. His Domain survival in Cap 2 is the first sign that the entity is operating above standard Nivel 3 parameters — a hit that nearly maxed Cho's redistribution ceiling is not a Nivel 3 hit.
