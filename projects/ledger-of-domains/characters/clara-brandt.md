## Domain — Implosion

Brandt generates a point of concentrated Odem compression within a defined range, causing an inward collapse — not explosion outward, but everything within the targeted volume drawn violently toward a single point. The collapse is not gravity; it is Odem pressure inverting on itself. Physical matter, Odem structures, entity coherence — all of it pulled to the center and compacted.

Range: approximately ten to fifteen meters for a controlled implosion. Larger radius means less precision and lower compression force. At close range, the effect is violent enough to destroy structural Odem patterns entirely.

**Standalone use:** viable against physical targets, constructions, entities with stable material form. She does not need Morales for standard engagements.

**Paired with Morales:** against entities whose coherence is maintained by Odem (Potestades, distributed presences), Morales must first nullify the Odem structure before Brandt's implosion has full effect. Without Morales, the implosion compresses physical matter but the Odem architecture can reconstruct around it. With Morales: the Odem architecture is already offline, and Brandt collapses what remains with no rebuild possible.

**Why it works against underground presences:** the implosion does not require line of sight — she generates the compression point at a location, not toward a target. Reyes maps the position, Brandt places the implosion point at those coordinates.

## Personality

Methodical. Comfortable with proximity to danger in a way that reads as indifference but is not. Eleven years in Hartford have given her very precise calibration of what is and is not an acceptable risk. Tends to close conversations rather than open them.

## Narrative function in BSA

Arc 1 (Hartford). Second half of the Morales–Brandt pair. Cap 2: implosión lanzada contra la Saturación directamente, tras que Morales no logró contacto. La Saturación la absorbió parcialmente — bordes del torso perdieron definición, se rehízo. Su masa de Odem fue suficiente para absorber la implosión y reconstituirse.
