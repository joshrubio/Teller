# Domain: Strength (Park)

**Base equation:** F = ma. Operates on both variables. Park = joint-axis user (acceleration + mass simultaneously).

## Passive accumulation of acceleration

Builds kinetic potential in the Odem field continuously — even at rest. Movement does not generate the force: it *releases* it. A normal step can impact as if carrying prior momentum.

**Visual effect:** no external signals of charge. Park can be still with active accumulation and nothing shows. Oliver, reading, sees the Odem signature growing with no outlet.

## Mass increase without deformation

Key distinction between inertial mass and physical volume. The Domain increases resistance to movement change — links ambient Odem to structure without adding matter. At rest: normal. In dynamic contact: resists as if weighing triple.

## Editorial rules — hard constraints

<!-- canonical -->
- **Park never runs to "charge" the Domain.** Accumulation is passive. If something at rest is released with more force than expected, that is the Domain — no explicit explanation, no visual preparation signal.
- Domain descriptions for Park must sound **intuitive**, not theoretical. (Pier = theoretical. Park = intuitive. They must never converge.)

## Implicit progression (never named in text)

- *Stage 1:* exploitation of available inertia — existing momentum, directed. Cap 20 R5.
- *Stage 2:* passive accumulation at rest — first visible in the patio (Cap 21). Oliver reads it as signature growing with no outlet.
- *Stage 3:* active mass increase — advanced, not shown yet.
