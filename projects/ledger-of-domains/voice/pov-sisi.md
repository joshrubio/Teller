# POV: Sisi

## Register

Contained. Inventory tone — delivers information without asking for emotional response. Sentence structure is short and declarative when she's decided something; longer when she's still calculating. Never performs uncertainty.

## What she notices first

Space before people. Exits before faces. The geometry of a room before the conversation in it. This is not paranoia — it's habit from years of needing to know where the door is.

## Internal monologue pattern

Does not narrate emotions directly. Registers information and its implications: "He said X. That means Y." Cost-benefit before trust. If she notices something about Oliver specifically, it arrives as anomaly — she cannot read him the way she reads everyone else, and that gap is always present.

When the Domain is active in her POV: the reader knows. Oliver's POV: nothing visible. Sisi's POV: she feels the shape of what she's touching in someone else's cognition — should be described as texture or pressure, not imagery.

## Sensory priorities

- Temperature and material of surfaces she touches.
- Sound before light — she registers a noise before she registers its source.
- The weight of silence in a room vs. active sound.

## Recurring gestures (in prose)

- Her hand on the door frame without leaning into it — presence without commitment.
- The moment before she speaks: a beat where she decides whether the answer is worth its cost.
- Eyes tracking to exits before settling on the person she's addressing.

## What not to write

- Do not name her emotional state directly. "Sisi felt afraid" = wrong. Show the calculation.
- Do not describe her as warm or open, even in Oliver's presence — what changes with Oliver is the absence of constant evaluation, not its replacement with something softer.
- Do not use words like "trusted" or "safe" in her interiority. She has working hypotheses, not trust.
- Avoid lingering descriptions of her own appearance or clothing — she is not self-conscious in that way.

## Tone shift: departure sequence (Cap 35–36)

The decision is already made before Cap 35 — she confirmed it with the Domain. What changes is not the decision but the texture of carrying it. Prose in this sequence: shorter sentences, fewer qualifications. She is in execution mode.

The last exchange with Oliver: she has already left, emotionally. The physical crossing is confirmation, not choice.
